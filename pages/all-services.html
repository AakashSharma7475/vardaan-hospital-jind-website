Pre-operative blood work

Laboratory panels tailored for orthopedic surgery: hemoglobin, white cell count, glucose, kidney function, and clotting studies when implants or major blood loss are expected.

Results are reviewed with anesthesia and your surgeon; if values are borderline, we reschedule or optimize care rather than proceeding blindly.

Hemoglobin optimization before joint replacement

Infection marker screening when hardware is involved

Diabetes and HbA1c checks for bone healing risk

Vitamin D and bone metabolism when indicated

Same-building phlebotomy for surgical patients

Digital results uploaded to your file before admission

MRI (musculoskeletal)

High-resolution imaging for knee, shoulder, hip, ankle, and spine—used to map ligament tears, meniscus injury, labral damage, disc herniation, and stress reactions.

Radiology reports are discussed in orthopedics the same week whenever possible, so you understand whether physiotherapy, injection, or surgery is the next step.

Region-specific protocols for athletes and laborers

Spine MRI with neural detail for sciatica

Metal-safety screening for implants

Contrast studies only when clinically justified

CD or cloud delivery to referring doctors

Correlation with weight-bearing X-rays when needed

Digital X-ray

Fast bone alignment studies for trauma, arthritis series, spine scoliosis screening, and post-operative hardware checks—with low dose and instant digital review.

Orthopedic surgeons rely on comparative views over time; keep prior films handy so we can judge healing, loosening, or progression of deformity.
