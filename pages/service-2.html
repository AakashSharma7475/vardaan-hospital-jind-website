Service Single #2

Magnetic Resonance Imaging

Orthopedic MRI for knees, shoulders, hips, and spine—with reports tied to your treatment plan

MRI shows ligaments, meniscus, labrum, cartilage, marrow, and neural elements. At VARDAAN Hospital Jind we use it to clarify sports injuries, workplace trauma, arthritis patterns, and spine disorders.

Your referring doctor may order a region-specific protocol; please mention implants, claustrophobia, or renal issues when booking so we can plan contrast and positioning safely.

After the scan, our orthopedic team reviews images alongside your examination—so decisions on bracing, physiotherapy, injection, or surgery stay aligned with what you actually need.

Dedicated musculoskeletal protocols for common injury patterns

Rapid communication with fracture and arthroscopy teams

Clear patient briefing before entering the scanner

Digital reports shared with your treating surgeon

Coordination with physiotherapy when rehab depends on MRI findings

Second-read options for complex spine and tumor mimic cases

Before and after your scan

Wear loose clothing without metal; arrive with prior CDs, films, or cloud links so serial comparison is possible.

Inform staff about pregnancy, pacemakers, or recent surgery—the technologist will confirm MRI safety.

Follow-up is booked with orthopedics once the structured report is ready; urgent findings are escalated the same day when clinically appropriate.
